Abstract

Learning a scene into disentangled shape, appearance, and illumination, is a fundamental problem in computer vision and graphics. With recent advancements in dif- ferentiable volume rendering and coordinate-based neural scene representations, neural approaches such as Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) have achieved remarkable perfor- mance. But existing NeRF and its followers fail to repro- duce high-quality rendering even very dense training views are given. Leveraging recent advancements in radiance fields reconstruction by vector-matrix (VM) decomposition, together with differentiable volume rendering approach that enables gradient-based optimization, we present a novel Neural Volumetric Frame (nvFrame) which jointly optimizes geometry, illumination, and texture from multi-view images observed under varying/same illuminations. In specific, nvFrame learns a set of compact neural basis which effec- tively spans the illumination and specular variation man- ifold observed in training views. The ray-casting direction and illumination input codes are effectively projected into a collective variational embedding space in which render- ing variations can be well decoded over the neural basis of nvFrame, which in turn facilitates the optimization of the basis interpretation and representation quality. Ex- periments show that our method can learn a single neural scene representation that renders novel views in varying or same illuminations and also achieves state-of-the-art per- formance on novel view synthesis.
